Leanne Bovey

Hi, my name is Leanne and I am a Fine Art student at Falmouth University, Cornwall. In my recent work, I'm aiming to renew the childlike sense of wonder and curiosity that we unknowingly leave behind as we approach adulthood by creating increasingly enigmatic and indeterminable objects.

Artist Statement

It’s time to regress back to our childhood, where we had a simpler view of the world, where we looked at everything with wonder, and approached the unknown with curiosity and our imaginations.


My work is an exploration of our natural neurological tendency to misinterpret patterns, also known as apophenia. We are particularly predisposed to find order, pattern, and meaning in the world, and therefore we often detect meaningful patterns which do not exist. Through my established language of form and colour created with ambiguous and indeterminable shapes, I’m exploring how this is an amusing aspect of our nature, one that can become a reason to rediscover the element of play. By giving the viewer the encouragement to determine these curious shapes in their own perception, their imagination is stimulated, and therefore play is encouraged.

Painting

Imagination is what makes our sensory experience meaningful, enabling us to interpret and make sense of what we do not initially understand. By painting amorphous and indeterminable forms, my aim is to challenge our visual interpretations, to rediscover creativity through the playful associations of unrecognisable visual stimuli. Challenging our minds and awakening our unconscious interferences that stimulate imaginative thinking, whilst working in bright pastels to evoke a sense of comfort and awaken our childlike wonder and curiosity.

Sculpture

My ongoing series of papier mâché sculptures aim to encourage our imaginative interpretations which are formed from our subconscious memory, which helps to determine the unrecognisable phenomena in the present.


We are made up of many parts and inside we still have the child we were with all its experiences and feelings. My ‘Unconscious Interference’ sculptures are hand-held, to stimulate imagination and play. As when we are being playful, spontaneous, creative and intuitive, our true self within us is being welcomed and encouraged to be present.

Digital Drawing

My graphic tablets drawings show my ongoing digital experimentations with pattern, layers, colour and curious forms that are inspired by the fundamental shapes that make up the world around us.

Object Series

My ‘Object Series’ is made up of painted plywood cut out forms. Ranging from 4 inches to 6 foot, these forms were created to be layered and arranged; as presented in my installation exhibition in the Woodlane Windows. This series transforms the viewer from mere spectator, to active creator, as they are invited to manipulate these interactive toys, the wooden material having an inviting element of childhood nostalgia while awakening creativity and sense of play.
